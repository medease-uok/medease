Abstract
The MedEase web application is designed to transform hospital management by providing a
comprehensive platform that streamlines administrative tasks, enhances patient care, and
optimizes resource allocation. This report details the journey of designing MedEase, a solution
specifically built to address the challenges faced by government hospitals by offering efficient
patient management, automated appointment scheduling, and real-time hospital resource tracking.
Through an in-depth analysis of the operational inefficiencies in traditional hospital management
systems, MedEase was conceptualized and developed to improve hospital workflows and enhance
communication between healthcare professionals, patients, and administrative staff. By integrating
key features such as electronic health records (EHR), appointment scheduling, patient queue
management, and automated report generation, the system minimizes manual processes, reduces
waiting times, and ensures seamless coordination within hospital departments.
The platform also enables real-time monitoring of bed availability, doctor schedules, and medical
inventory, ensuring hospitals can allocate resources effectively. Additionally, patients can access
their medical records and track appointment statuses, promoting transparency and improved
healthcare accessibility.
This report presents the current progress of the MedEase project, covering the project background,
system analysis through diagrams, requirement specifications, user interface design, and
architectural design. Ultimately, MedEase aims to revolutionize hospital administration by
leveraging technology to enhance efficiency, reduce administrative burdens, and improve overall
patient care. The project highlights how innovation can modernize government hospital
management, ensuring a smarter and more accessible healthcare experience for all stakeholders.

Chapter 1 - Introduction
This chapter introduces the proposed system, its background, and the challenges it seeks to address.
It also defines the objectives, aims, scope, and boundaries of the system, providing a foundation
for understanding the purpose and impact of MedEase.
1.1 Project Background
MedEase is a web application designed to enhance the efficiency of government hospital
management by streamlining administrative tasks, improving patient care coordination, and
optimizing hospital resources. Government hospitals often face significant challenges in managing
patient records, scheduling appointments, and allocating medical resources due to outdated manual
processes and insufficient digital infrastructure. These inefficiencies lead to long patient wait
times, misplaced medical records, and difficulty in coordinating hospital operations.
By introducing MedEase, we aim to provide a centralized, automated hospital management system
that integrates patient registration, appointment scheduling, medical record management, and realtime resource tracking. This solution minimizes manual workloads for hospital staff, enhances
communication between departments, and improves overall hospital efficiency.
The platform ensures real-time access to patient data, automated queue management, and seamless
coordination between doctors, nurses, and administrative staff. Additionally, MedEase supports
government hospitals by maintaining compliance with healthcare regulations, reducing
paperwork, and enabling data-driven decision-making to improve patient care quality.
By addressing key pain points in hospital administration, MedEase aims to enhance operational
efficiency, reduce patient waiting times, and ensure a seamless healthcare experience for both
medical professionals and patients. The implementation of this system will contribute to the
modernization of public healthcare services, ultimately improving accessibility and healthcare
outcomes for communities.

1.2 Problem Definition
Government hospitals in Sri Lanka face significant challenges in managing patient care efficiently due to
outdated and manual hospital management processes. These inefficiencies lead to long waiting times,
overburdened medical staff, and inadequate resource utilization, affecting both patient experience and
healthcare quality. The key problems include:
•

Extensive Patient Waiting Times
o

Patients often face long queues for consultations, diagnostic tests, and treatments due to
the high patient-to-doctor ratio.

o

Waiting times can stretch for hours or even days, causing significant distress, particularly
for critical patients.

•

Overburdened Medical Staff
o

Doctors, nurses, and healthcare professionals struggle with excessive workloads due to the
high volume of patients.

o

The strain on hospital staff often results in burnout, reduced quality of care, and
administrative inefficiencies.

•

Lack of Digital Hospital Management Systems
o

Many hospitals still rely on paper-based records, leading to inefficiencies, misplaced
documents, and medical errors.

o

The absence of electronic health records (EHR), online appointment systems, and
automated patient management increases operational challenges.

•

Limited Accessibility and Communication Barriers
o

Patients, especially those from rural areas, often travel long distances for treatment, only
to face delays due to poor hospital coordination.

o

The lack of real-time communication between patients, doctors, and hospital
administration further worsens the situation, making healthcare services less accessible.

MedEase addresses these critical issues by offering an automated hospital management system that
streamlines patient registration, appointment scheduling, medical record management, and hospital
resource tracking. Through digital transformation, MedEase aims to reduce waiting times, enhance staff
efficiency, and improve overall healthcare accessibility for government hospitals in Sri Lanka.

1.3 Aims and Objectives
1.3.2 Aim
The main aim of this project is to develop a web-based hospital management system that enhances patient
care, optimizes hospital operations, and improves communication between patients, doctors, hospital staff,
and pharmacists. MedEase provides a seamless digital platform to reduce waiting times, streamline patient
record management, and facilitate better access to healthcare services.
1.3.2 Objectives
For Patients:
•

Enable patients to browse available doctors and schedule appointments conveniently.

•

Provide real-time updates on appointment statuses, consultation schedules, and test results.

•

Improve patient experience with a simple UI and easy navigation for seamless interaction.

•

Facilitate access to medicine and laboratory services from different sellers.

For Doctors:
•

Allow doctors to store and access patient medical records and history, ensuring efficient and
informed decision-making.

•

Provide a centralized platform to manage patient interactions, prescriptions, and follow-ups.

For Hospital Staff:
•

Optimize hospital resource management to minimize overcrowding and enhance operational
efficiency.

•

Digitize patient record-keeping to reduce paperwork and eliminate manual errors.

•

Enable staff communication through an internal system for seamless coordination.

For Pharmacists:
•

Offer a platform where pharmacies can advertise themselves and expand their business.

•

Connect with patients across Sri Lanka, providing convenient access to medications and healthcare
products.

By integrating these functionalities, MedEase aims to modernize government hospitals, improving patient
experience, operational efficiency, and accessibility to medical services while leveraging technology for
better healthcare outcomes.

1.4 Scope and Boundaries
The scope of this project is focused on the development of an advanced hospital management system,
MedEase, designed specifically for government hospitals. The system aims to enhance patient care,
appointment scheduling, hospital resource management, and communication among healthcare
professionals through a web-based platform.
1.4.1 Key Features Within Scope
User Management: Support for multiple user roles, including patients, doctors, hospital staff, and
pharmacists, each with dedicated features tailored to their needs.
Appointment Scheduling: Patients can browse doctors, schedule appointments, and receive real-time
updates on their consultation status.
Electronic Medical Records (EMR): Doctors can store and access patient medical history, improving
diagnosis accuracy and treatment efficiency.
Hospital Resource Optimization: Hospital staff can manage patient flow, reduce overcrowding, and
streamline hospital operations using automated tools.
Pharmacy Integration: Pharmacists can advertise their services, connect with patients, and facilitate easy
access to medications.
Internal Communication System: A secure communication channel for doctors, nurses, and hospital staff
to enhance coordination and operational efficiency.
1.4.2 Boundaries of the Project
The primary focus of MedEase is on hospital operations, patient management, and healthcare accessibility.
However, certain functionalities are beyond the current scope, including:
•

Advanced AI-driven diagnosis systems (MedEase will provide structured medical records but not
AI-powered disease predictions at this stage).

•

Integration with third-party payment systems for online billing and insurance claims.

•

Telemedicine functionalities, such as video consultations and remote patient monitoring, which
may be considered in future phases.

•

Deep analytical insights for hospital performance tracking beyond basic reporting functionalities.

MedEase is designed as a scalable and modular system, ensuring that future enhancements can be
incorporated to meet the evolving needs of government hospitals.

Chapter 2 - System Analysis
2.1 Fact Gathering Techniques Used
To develop MedEase, a hospital management system aimed at reducing patient waiting times, improving
hospital resource utilization, and enhancing communication between healthcare professionals, we
conducted thorough research and fact-gathering. These techniques helped us identify user requirements,
understand challenges in hospital management, and ensure the system effectively addresses real-world
problems. The fact-gathering techniques used include:
2.1.1 Brainstorming Sessions
We conducted brainstorming sessions with hospital administrators, doctors, nurses, patients, and
software developers to gain insights into the existing inefficiencies in hospital operations. These
sessions helped us:
o

Identify critical pain points in the current hospital workflow, including long queues,
appointment mismanagement, and lack of patient record accessibility.

o

Define essential features that would make hospital operations smoother, such as real-time
appointment scheduling, patient record management, and pharmacy access.

o

Explore technological solutions to address overcrowding, inefficient resource allocation,
and patient-doctor communication gaps.

o

Understand the expectations of hospital staff, ensuring that the system is user-friendly and
integrates seamlessly with their daily workflow.

Key Findings from Brainstorming Sessions:
o

Doctors and nurses need quick access to patient medical histories without relying on
physical files.

o

Patients prefer an easy-to-use appointment booking system to minimize hospital wait
times.

o

Hospital staff require a structured system to manage bed availability, patient records, and
medical prescriptions.

o

Pharmacists would benefit from a system that connects them with hospitals for better
inventory and prescription management.

7

2.1.2 Interviews with Key Stakeholders
To gain a firsthand understanding of the challenges faced by different users, we conducted
structured interviews with:
o

Patients (both urban and rural)

o

Doctors and nurses working in government and private hospitals

o

Hospital administrative staff handling patient records and appointments

o

Pharmacists supplying medicine to hospitals and patients

The interviews focused on:
o

Current appointment scheduling processes and the difficulties patients face when trying to
get a consultation.

o

How doctors and hospital staff manage patient data and what problems arise due to manual
processes.

o

Pharmacists’ challenges in handling prescriptions and ensuring patients get their
medications on time.

o

Communication gaps between different departments in hospitals and their impact on
efficiency.

Key Findings from Interviews:
o

Patients struggle with long waiting times due to inefficient scheduling and lack of real-time
updates.

o

Doctors face difficulties accessing patient histories, as many hospitals still rely on paper
records.

o

Hospital staff are overwhelmed with administrative work, leading to mismanagement of
resources.

o

Pharmacists lack a centralized system for managing prescriptions and stock availability.

2.1.3 Research and Competitive Analysis / Benchmarking
To understand how existing hospital management systems work and what improvements MedEase
could offer, we conducted a competitive analysis of existing solutions in both Sri Lanka and
internationally.
We analyzed:
o

Existing hospital management software to identify gaps and areas of improvement.

o

Appointment scheduling systems used in private hospitals and whether they meet patient
needs.

8

o

Electronic medical record (EMR) systems and how they help hospitals streamline
operations.

o

Pharmacy management systems and their integration with hospital databases.

Key Findings from Competitive Analysis:
o

Most hospitals in Sri Lanka do not have a centralized system for managing appointments,
medical records, and prescriptions.

o

Some private hospitals use basic appointment scheduling software, but it does not support
real-time updates or doctor availability tracking.

o

There is limited communication between hospitals, pharmacies, and patients, leading to
delays in medication access.

o

Many existing systems lack mobile accessibility, making it difficult for patients to check
appointments and prescriptions remotely.

2.1.4 Observations
We conducted direct observations at hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies to study how:
o

Patients interact with appointment scheduling systems (or the lack thereof).

o

Doctors and nurses manage patient records and workflows in real-world hospital
environments.

o

Hospital staff handle administrative processes, including patient registration and
prescription documentation.

o

Pharmacists process and verify prescriptions from hospitals and clinics.

Key Findings from Observations:
o

Patients often travel long distances only to face delays and appointment cancellations due
to mismanagement.

o

Many patients, especially elderly individuals, struggle with complex hospital processes,
highlighting the need for a simple, guided digital system.

o

Doctors waste significant time retrieving patient history manually, impacting consultation
efficiency.

o

Pharmacists frequently face stock shortages, and there is no real-time tracking of medicine
availability between hospitals and pharmacies.

2.1.5 Expert Consultation
To ensure that MedEase is built with medical industry standards and best practices, we consulted
healthcare professionals and IT experts specializing in hospital management systems. These experts
provided insights into:
o

Data security and compliance with healthcare regulations such as patient confidentiality
and electronic health record (EHR) standards.

o

Scalability of the system, ensuring that it can be deployed across different hospital
departments and even multiple locations.

o

Integration of laboratory, pharmacy, and emergency care units to improve coordination
between various hospital services.

o

User experience (UX) design considerations for hospital staff, ensuring that the system is
intuitive and efficient.

o

Technical challenges in implementing real-time updates for patient records, doctor
availability, and medicine stock levels.

Key Findings from Expert Consultation:
o

Data privacy is a critical concern and must comply with local healthcare regulations.

o

The system must handle high traffic loads during peak hours in hospitals without
performance issues.

o

A modular design approach is recommended to allow different hospitals to customize
features based on their needs.

o

Doctors need voice-to-text capabilities for faster record-keeping during consultations.

o

Hospital IT teams need an easy way to manage and update records without disrupting
hospital operations.

To fulfill the hospital management requirements of Medease, several alternative approaches were

Manual Record-Keeping (Traditional Paper-Based System)
and clinics can use paper-based records to track patient details, appointments, and billing. While

•

Using Multiple Specialized Software Solutions
Hospitals may adopt separate software solutions for different functions, such as one system for

maintenance costs.

Web-Based Hospital Management System (Medease Approach)
A cloud-based hospital management platform like Medease offers a centralized solution that

making it the most efficient solution.

•

Using Third-Party Healthcare Platforms
Hospitals can rely on external platforms that offer electronic health record (EHR) management,
appointment scheduling, and billing. While this reduces the need for in-house infrastructure, it may
lead to dependency on third-party providers, limited customization, and potential privacy concerns
regarding sensitive patient data.

Telemedicine and Mobile Health Apps
Instead of a comprehensive hospital management system, hospitals could use telemedicine and

Open-Source Hospital Management Systems
Hospitals can implement open-source hospital management software to avoid licensing fees. While
this offers flexibility and cost savings, it requires technical expertise for customization,

3.2.1 Description of Architectural Concerns for Medease
The high-level software architecture for Medease is carefully designed to prioritize scalability,
maintainability, and flexibility. At its core, Medease adopts a microservices architecture to ensure that
different components of the hospital management system can scale independently while remaining
seamlessly connected. By leveraging AWS for cloud infrastructure and Helapay for payment integration,
Medease aims to provide a highly reliable and efficient system that can support the demands of modern
healthcare environments. Key elements of the architecture include,
•

Microservices Architecture:
Medease utilizes a microservices-based approach, where each service is responsible for distinct
functionality such as patient records management, appointment scheduling, medication tracking,
and lab test reports. This modular architecture allows teams to work independently on different
modules, speeding up development cycles and reducing the impact of changes or issues within

53

individual services. The microservices are loosely coupled, ensuring that each one can evolve
without disrupting the overall system. This enables efficient scaling based on user load, with new
services or features being easily integrated into the ecosystem.
•

API Gateway and Load Balancer:
At the entry point of the Medease system, the Amazon API Gateway efficiently manages client
requests, ensuring they are routed to the correct microservices. This API Gateway provides security
features such as rate limiting, authentication, and logging, allowing for easy management of API
calls. Additionally, Elastic Load Balancing (ELB) ensures that incoming traffic is evenly distributed
across multiple microservice instances, optimizing resource utilization and always ensuring high
availability of the application.

•

Serverless Components:
Medease leverages AWS Lambda to handle specific tasks that don’t require a constant server
presence, such as processing patient registration, triggering notifications, and updating appointment
statuses. This serverless approach minimizes operational overhead, allowing the system to
automatically scale based on demand and providing cost efficiency. Lambda functions are
particularly useful for tasks like generating medical reports, managing patient consent forms, and
verifying insurance details, all of which can be handled asynchronously in the background.

•

Event-Driven Architecture:
Medease’s architecture thrives on an event-driven model using AWS SNS (Simple Notification
Service) and SQS (Simple Queue Service) for real-time notifications and service communication.
For example, when a new lab report is uploaded or a doctor’s schedule is updated, events are
triggered to inform the respective users—whether patients, doctors, or administrative staff. This
decouples services and allows the system to react dynamically to changing conditions, improving
system efficiency and responsiveness.

•

Database Layer:
To manage patient data, medical records, and appointments, Medease uses a hybrid database
strategy. Amazon DynamoDB is used to store critical, high-velocity data such as patient
appointments, hospital staff schedules, and real-time availability of resources. Meanwhile, Amazon
RDS (Relational Database Service) manages structured medical records, prescriptions, and billing
information. This combination ensures both speed and reliability, with seamless integration

54

between transactional and non-transactional data. Data consistency and high performance are
maintained using distributed database technologies and automated backups.
•

Helapay Integration for Payments:
Medease integrates Helapay for payment processing, offering secure and seamless financial
transactions between patients, healthcare providers, and the hospital system. Helapay ensures
smooth payment flows for services such as consultation fees, lab tests, and medical prescriptions,
all while maintaining the highest standards of security and compliance. This integration simplifies
the payment process for users, reducing friction and enhancing the overall user experience.

•

Security and Authentication:
For the security and privacy of sensitive medical data, Medease incorporates an advanced
authentication system via AWS Cognito. This service handles user identity management,
authentication, and authorization with support for multi-factor authentication (MFA), ensuring that
only authorized users—patients, doctors, nurses, and admins—can access specific parts of the
system. Role-based access control (RBAC) ensures that different user groups have appropriate
permissions aligned with their responsibilities, such as a doctor accessing medical records and a
nurse updating patient vitals.

•

Maintainability and Extensibility:
Medease is built with long-term maintainability in mind. The use of microservices and serverless
components means that each service is independently deployable, making it easy to update and
scale specific parts of the system without affecting others. Additionally, new features or services
can be added with minimal disruption to the existing infrastructure. The architecture is designed to
support horizontal scaling, enabling the system to handle increased user activity and data volume
seamlessly as the hospital expands or as new features are introduced.

•

External Integrations:
Google Calendar Integration: Medease integrates with Google Calendar to help doctors and patients
manage appointments and schedules, providing real-time updates on availability and reducing
scheduling conflicts.
Helapay Integration: Secure, hassle-free payments for consultations, medical services, and hospital
bills.
SMS and Email Notifications: Automated notifications are sent to patients, doctors, and hospital
staff for appointment reminders, prescription updates, and lab report availability.

55

•

Scalability and Performance:
With AWS as the backbone, Medease ensures that the system can scale effortlessly both vertically
and horizontally. AWS’s elasticity allows the system to automatically adjust to changes in user
demand—whether it’s a surge in traffic during peak hospital hours or an increase in data volume
during patient admissions. The use of Elastic Beanstalk for managed deployment and Amazon EC2
Auto Scaling ensures optimal resource usage, while Amazon CloudFront helps deliver content
faster to users across the globe.

•

Cloud-Agnostic Design:
While Medease leverages AWS for cloud services, the architecture is designed to be cloud-agnostic.
If the infrastructure were to migrate to another cloud provider, such as Google Cloud Platform
(GCP) or Microsoft Azure, the transition would be seamless. The microservices, APIs, and data
storage solutions are abstracted in such a way that they could be easily adapted to another platform
with minimal rework.

The architecture of Medease is designed to ensure optimal performance, high availability, and ease of use.
By leveraging AWS cloud-native services and integrating modern technologies like Helapay, the system
can efficiently handle the complexities of hospital management, from patient data management to secure
financial transactions. With an event-driven, scalable, and flexible architecture, Medease is well-positioned
to adapt to future growth while delivering exceptional care to patients and streamlined operations for
healthcare providers.

This chapter provides a comprehensive summary of the key findings and insights derived from the
development of the Medease hospital management system. It evaluates the extent to which the project’s

limitations encountered during development, and outlines potential future improvements to enhance its

4.1 Degree of Objectives Met
The primary objective of the Medease hospital management system was to create an efficient, user-friendly,
and scalable digital platform for managing hospital operations. The system was designed to streamline
processes such as patient registration, appointment scheduling, medical record management, prescription
handling, and laboratory result management.
Through rigorous development and testing, Medease successfully met its objectives by implementing key
features that optimize hospital workflow. The system allows seamless interaction between patients, doctors,
lab technicians, and administrative staff, ensuring that medical services are delivered in a structured and
organized manner.
Additionally, Medease integrates secure authentication and authorization mechanisms, ensuring that only
authorized personnel can access sensitive medical data. The incorporation of cloud-based storage and
processing further enhances the system’s efficiency, ensuring that all data is readily available and secure.
User feedback gathered during the prototype phase indicated that the system aligns well with the needs of
hospitals and healthcare professionals. Patients appreciated the ease of booking appointments and accessing
medical records, while doctors and administrators found the platform useful for managing patient
information and appointments. This validation underscores the effectiveness of the design and
implementation strategies employed throughout the development process.

4.2 Usability, Accessibility, Reliability, and Friendliness
Medease has been built with a strong emphasis on usability and accessibility, ensuring that individuals from
diverse backgrounds, including those with limited technical expertise, can navigate the system effortlessly.

Accessibility features, such as support for screen readers, high-contrast modes, and keyboard navigation,

regardless of physical or technological constraints, can interact with the system efficiently.
Reliability is a critical factor in hospital management, as healthcare professionals and patients depend on
accurate and timely information. Medease employs cloud computing solutions that provide high availability

event of technical failures. Additionally, robust data encryption and backup mechanisms have been

The system’s user-friendliness extends beyond its interface, as Medease incorporates automated reminders

enhancing workflow automation, Medease contributes to a more organized and stress-free healthcare
environment.

4.3 Limitations and Drawbacks
Despite the successes achieved, certain limitations and drawbacks were identified during the development
and testing phases. One of the key challenges is handling many concurrent users, especially in high-traffic
hospital environments. While the system performs efficiently under normal conditions, performance
optimizations may be required to ensure scalability as the number of users increases.
Another limitation is the dependency on cloud-based services such as AWS, which, while offering
scalability and security, may introduce challenges if there is a need to migrate to a different cloud provider.
Vendor lock-in could pose financial and technical constraints in the long run, necessitating careful planning
for future expansion.
Additionally, although Medease has been developed with modularity in mind, the integration of new
features may require thorough testing to ensure compatibility with existing functionalities. Expanding the
system to support additional hospital services, such as telemedicine or emergency response tracking, may
introduce complexities that need to be addressed through careful architectural planning.

To further enhance the capabilities of Medease, several modifications and improvements are planned for

insurance claim processing, would enhance convenience for both patients and hospital administrators.
A promising future extension is the incorporation of AI-driven chatbots and virtual assistants to assist
patients in booking appointments, answering general health inquiries, and providing medication reminders.

Another crucial enhancement is the development of a mobile application for Medease. While the web-based
platform is fully responsive and accessible on mobile devices, a dedicated mobile app would provide a
more seamless experience, enabling patients and doctors to access information on the go.
Finally, integrating blockchain technology for secure patient record management is a potential long-term
enhancement. Blockchain could provide an immutable and transparent record of patient data, ensuring
enhanced security, data integrity, and interoperability across multiple healthcare institutions.
